Electric Dipole 

Two opposite charges with magnitude qqq are placed at a distance ddd from each other. Calculate the electric field at the distance aaa from the midpoint. This point is at a distance rrr from both charges:

r=(d2)2+a2=d2+4a24=d2+4a22. \begin{align*} r &= \sqrt{\left(\frac{d}{2}\right)^2 + a^2} \\ &= \sqrt{\frac{d^2 + 4a^2}{4}} \\ &= \frac{\sqrt{d^2 + 4a^2}}{2}. \\ \end{align*} r​=(2d​)2+a2​=4d2+4a2​​=2d2+4a2​​.​

Let r1⃗\vec{r_1}r1​​ be the position of the first charge, r2⃗\vec{r_2}r2​​ the position of the second charge and rt⃗\vec{r_t}rt​​ the at which the electric field is calculated:

r=∣rt⃗−r1⃗∣=∣rt⃗−r2⃗∣,d=∣r1⃗−r2⃗∣,r^s1=r⃗t−r⃗1r,r^s2=r⃗t−r⃗2r. \begin{align*} r &= |\vec{r_t} - \vec{r_1}| = |\vec{r_t} - \vec{r_2}|, \\ d &= |\vec{r_1} - \vec{r_2}|, \\ \hat{r}_{s1} &= \frac{\vec{r}_t - \vec{r}_1}{r}, \\ \hat{r}_{s2} &= \frac{\vec{r}_t - \vec{r}_2}{r}. \end{align*} rdr^s1​r^s2​​=∣rt​​−r1​​∣=∣rt​​−r2​​∣,=∣r1​​−r2​​∣,=rrt​−r1​​,=rrt​−r2​​.​

The total electric field, E⃗\vec{E}E is equal to:

E⃗=E⃗1+E⃗2=keqr3(r⃗t−r⃗1)−keqr3(r⃗t−r⃗2)=keqr3(r⃗t−r⃗1−r⃗t+r⃗2)=keqr3(r⃗2−r⃗1)=keq(d2+4a22)3(r⃗2−r⃗1)=8keq(d2+4a2)32(r⃗2−r⃗1). \begin{align*} \vec{E} &= \vec{E}_1 + \vec{E}_2 \\ &= k_e \frac{q}{r^3} (\vec{r}_t - \vec{r}_1) - k_e \frac{q}{r^3} (\vec{r}_t - \vec{r}_2) \\ &= k_e \frac{q}{r^3} (\vec{r}_t - \vec{r}_1 - \vec{r}_t + \vec{r}_2) \\ &= k_e \frac{q}{r^3} (\vec{r}_2 - \vec{r}_1) \\ &= k_e \frac{q}{\left(\frac{\sqrt{d^2 + 4a^2}}{2}\right)^3} (\vec{r}_2 - \vec{r}_1) \\ &= \frac{8 k_e q}{(d^2 + 4a^2)^{\frac{3}{2}}} (\vec{r}_2 - \vec{r}_1). \end{align*} E​=E1​+E2​=ke​r3q​(rt​−r1​)−ke​r3q​(rt​−r2​)=ke​r3q​(rt​−r1​−rt​+r2​)=ke​r3q​(r2​−r1​)=ke​(2d2+4a2​​)3q​(r2​−r1​)=(d2+4a2)23​8ke​q​(r2​−r1​).​
