Electric Potential and Voltage

The electric potential is the potential energy per unit charge. When we put into an electric field a charge of value qqq, the electric potential is equal to:

φ=Epq.\varphi = \frac{E_p}{q}.φ=qEp​​.

The unit of electric potential is volt (VVV).

The work the electric field does is equal to:

W=∫CFe⋅ds=−Wext,W = \int_C \boldsymbol{F_e} \cdot d\boldsymbol{s} = -W_{ext},W=∫C​Fe​⋅ds=−Wext​,

where WextW_{ext}Wext​ is the external work. The work needed to move a particle.

The potential energy is equal to the work required to move a charge qqq from an infinite distance to a position described by the displacement vector r\boldsymbol{r}r:

Ep=Wext=−∫CFe⋅ds=−∫CqE⋅ds. \begin{align*} E_p = W_{ext} &= -\int_C \boldsymbol{F_e} \cdot d\boldsymbol{s} \\ &= -\int_C q \boldsymbol{E} \cdot d\boldsymbol{s}. \\ \end{align*} Ep​=Wext​​=−∫C​Fe​⋅ds=−∫C​qE⋅ds.​

The separation vector s\boldsymbol{s}s described by radial coordinates is equal to:

s(t)=trxrx^+tryry^,s(t)=t,s^(t)=rxrx^+ryry^,s′(t)=rxrx^+ryry^,r≤t≤∞. \begin{align*} \boldsymbol{s}(t) &= t \frac{r_x}{r} \boldsymbol{\hat{x}} + t \frac{r_y}{r} \boldsymbol{\hat{y}}, \\ s(t) &= t, \\ \boldsymbol{\hat{s}}(t) &= \frac{r_x}{r} \boldsymbol{\hat{x}} + \frac{r_y}{r} \boldsymbol{\hat{y}}, \\ \boldsymbol{s'}(t) &= \frac{r_x}{r} \boldsymbol{\hat{x}} + \frac{r_y}{r} \boldsymbol{\hat{y}}, \\ r &\leq t \leq \infty. \end{align*} s(t)s(t)s^(t)s′(t)r​=trrx​​x^+trry​​y^​,=t,=rrx​​x^+rry​​y^​,=rrx​​x^+rry​​y^​,≤t≤∞.​

By the gradient theorem:

E=−∇φ,\boldsymbol{E} = - \boldsymbol{\nabla} \varphi,E=−∇φ,

meaning the electric field points into the direction of steepest decrease in electric potential.

The voltage is the difference in potentials:

U=φ2−φ1=keQ(1r2−1r1). \begin{align*} U &= \varphi_2 - \varphi_1 \\ &= k_e Q \left(\frac{1}{r_2} - \frac{1}{r_1}\right). \end{align*} U​=φ2​−φ1​=ke​Q(r2​1​−r1​1​).​

or work per unit charge:

U=−1q∫CFe⋅ds=−Wq=Wextq, \begin{align*} U &= -\frac{1}{q} \int_C \boldsymbol{F_e} \cdot d\boldsymbol{s} \\ &= -\frac{W}{q} \\ &= \frac{W_{ext}}{q}, \end{align*} U​=−q1​∫C​Fe​⋅ds=−qW​=qWext​​,​

Potential in a Radial Electric Field 

A point of charge QQQ is creating a radial electric field. Calculate the electric potential at point AAA.

The displacement between AAA and QQQ is equal to:

r=A−Q,\boldsymbol{r} = A - Q,r=A−Q,

then:

φ=keQr.\varphi = \frac{k_e Q}{r}.φ=rke​Q​.

What is the work required to move a charge qqq from a point with potential φ1\varphi_1φ1​ to a point with potential φ2\varphi_2φ2​?

U=Wextq=φ2−φ1,Wext=q(φ2−φ1). \begin{align*} U &= \frac{W_{ext}}{q} = \varphi_2 - \varphi_1, \\ W_{ext} &= q (\varphi_2 - \varphi_1). \end{align*} UWext​​=qWext​​=φ2​−φ1​,=q(φ2​−φ1​).​
