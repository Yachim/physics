Electric Potential

The electric potential is the potential energy per unit charge. When we put into an electric field a charge of value qqq, the electric potential is equal to:

φ=Epq.\varphi = \frac{E_p}{q}.φ=qEp​​.

The unit of electric potential is volt (VVV).

The voltage can be defined as the difference in potential energies:

Work Required to Move From One Potential to Another 

What is the work required to move a charge qqq from a point with potential φ1\varphi_1φ1​ to a point with potential φ2\varphi_2φ2​?

The work can be derived from the voltage:

U=Wq=φ2−φ1,W=q(φ2−φ1). \begin{align*} U &= \frac{W}{q} = \varphi_2 - \varphi_1, \\ W &= q (\varphi_2 - \varphi_1). \end{align*} UW​=qW​=φ2​−φ1​,=q(φ2​−φ1​).​

Potential in a Radial Electric Field 

A point of charge QQQ is creating a radial electric field. Calculate the electric potential at point AAA.

The displacement between AAA and QQQ is equal to:

r=A−Q. \begin{align*} \boldsymbol{r} &= A - Q. \end{align*} r​=A−Q.​

Reusing the electric potential derived from work:

φ=keQr.\varphi = \frac{k_e Q}{r}.φ=rke​Q​.
