Resistivity, Resistance, Conductivity, Conductance

Resistivity (ρ\rhoρ) is a property of a material that measures its ability to resist the electric current. The unit of resistivity is ohm meter (Ωm\Omega mΩm).

Conductivity is the reciprocal of resistivity:

σ=1ρ,\sigma = \frac{1}{\rho},σ=ρ1​,

the unit of conductivity is siemens per meter (Sm−1S m^{-1}Sm−1).

The resistance RRR and conductance GGG of a conductor is equal to:

R=ρlA,G=σAl=1R, \begin{align*} R &= \rho \frac{l}{A}, \\ G &= \sigma \frac{A}{l} = \frac{1}{R}, \end{align*} RG​=ρAl​,=σlA​=R1​,​

where lll is the length of the conductor and AAA is the cross-sectional area.

The unit of resistance is ohm (Ω\OmegaΩ) and the unit of conductance is siemens (SSS).
