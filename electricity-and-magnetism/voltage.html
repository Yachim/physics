Voltage

If we insert a charge q into an electric field, an electric force will start to act on it and it will start moving it. The work WWW is given by:

W=∫CFe⋅ds=∫CqE⋅ds. \begin{align*} W &= \int_C \boldsymbol{F_e} \cdot d\boldsymbol{s} \\ &= \int_C q \boldsymbol{E} \cdot d\boldsymbol{s}. \end{align*} W​=∫C​Fe​⋅ds=∫C​qE⋅ds.​

The voltage is defined as the amount of work per unit charge:

U=Wq=1q∫CqE⋅ds=∫CE⋅ds, \begin{align*} U &= \frac{W}{q} \\ &= \frac{1}{q} \int_C q \boldsymbol{E} \cdot d\boldsymbol{s} \\ &= \int_C \boldsymbol{E} \cdot d\boldsymbol{s}, \end{align*} U​=qW​=q1​∫C​qE⋅ds=∫C​E⋅ds,​

The unit of electric potential is volt (VVV).

or if the electric field is not variable:

U=E⋅s=Escos⁡θ. \begin{align*} U &= \boldsymbol{E} \cdot \boldsymbol{s} \\ &= Es \cos \theta. \end{align*} U​=E⋅s=Escosθ.​
