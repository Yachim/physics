Proper Length - Circumference 

For the proper length of circumference, we will take t,r,θt, r, \thetat,r,θ to be constant. The metric simplifies:

ds2=r2dϕ2.ds^2 = r^2 d\phi^2.ds2=r2dϕ2.

The rrr coordinate accurately gives the proper length of the circumference. But for the distance from the center the proper length is longer the closer an observer is to the object.
